Scientific Definitions

Delta

River-dominated estuaries with extensive sediment deposition forming distributary networks at the river mouth. Characterized by high sediment load and active land-building processes.

Fjord

Deep, narrow inlets carved by glacial erosion, typically with steep sides and significant depth. Common in high-latitude regions with historical glaciation (Norway, Chile, New Zealand).

Lagoon

Shallow coastal water bodies separated from the ocean by barrier islands, sandbars, or coral reefs. Generally characterized by restricted circulation and variable salinity.

Ria

Drowned river valleys formed by sea-level rise, typically V-shaped in cross-section. Deeper than typical coastal plain estuaries, with branching patterns following original river tributaries.

Coastal Plain

Wide, shallow estuaries formed in low-relief coastal areas. Often funnel-shaped with gradually sloping bottoms, common on passive continental margins.

Bar-Built

Estuaries formed behind barrier islands, spits, or offshore bars created by wave action and longshore sediment transport. Protected from direct ocean wave energy.

Tectonic

Estuaries formed by geological faulting, subsidence, or other tectonic processes. Structure controlled by underlying geological features rather than erosion or deposition.
